Definition

Sinus bradycardia occurs when the heart's natural pacemaker, the sinus node, generates electrical signals slower than a normal rate. It results in a heart rate of less than 60 beats per minute in adults.

Medical indications

Some medications can help increase heart rate, such as beta-agonists, alpha-2 adrenergic receptor agonists, and catecholamine reuptake inhibitors.

Control and recommendations

Check your heart rate regularly. Either by taking your pulse or using a portable heart monitor. If the heart rate is low, steps should be taken to increase it, such as resting, drinking fluids, and avoiding strenuous physical activity.

Diagnostic

The diagnosis is made through an electrocardiogram.

Treatment

If symptoms are present or the heart rate is too slow and causing problems, treatment may include medications or a pacemaker implantation.

Definition

Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT) is a rapid heart rate originating above the ventricles, often in the atria or atrioventricular node. It can result in a heart rate significantly higher than the normal range.

Medical indications

Medications that help regulate the heart rhythm, such as beta blockers, calcium channel blockers, antiarrhythmics, or consider cardiac ablation.

Control and recommendations

Monitor your heart rate regularly. Either by taking your pulse or using a portable heart monitor. If your heart rate is irregular, you should rest, drink fluids, and avoid strenuous physical activity.

Diagnostic

The diagnosis is made through an electrocardiogram.

Treatment

Treatment options include vagal maneuvers (e.g., bearing down, carotid sinus massage) to try to terminate the arrhythmia. Medications like adenosine, beta-blockers, or calcium channel blockers may be used to control or prevent SVT. Catheter ablation is a curative treatment option for some types of SVT.
